Siwa Desert

Siwa is one of the depressions of the Western Desert that formed a green oasis over an area of 10​88 km.

Safari in Siwa Desert

One of the most important places for safari trips is the Stone Forest. The trees in this forest are 40 million years old and have turned into stones. It is located in the southeast of Siwa, 20 kilometers from Bir Wahda.

Sulphur springs in Siwa

It is known that sulfur water treats many diseases that may affect the human body. In fact, Siwa has approximately 200 natural sulfur springs, the most famous of which are “Kebetra, Qut, Dakrur, Trishtjzer, Fatnas, and Al-Hamam.”

Ras Mohammed Reserve

Ras Mohammed is an Egyptian nature reserve in South Sinai, specifically 12 km from Sharm el-Sheikh. It borders the Gulf of Aqaba to the east and the Gulf of Suez to the west. The nature reserve was established in 1983. Diving and swimming are popular activities in Ras Mohammed.

Ras Mohammed Reserve

Ras Mohammed is home to 1,000 miles of coral reef, 200,000 fish species, and over 1,000 species of marine life. The reefs are 200 meters deep and 15 meters wide. They are also known as the "Golden Reefs of the World."

Luxor & Aswan

Luxor and Aswan are two cities in Upper Egypt located on the banks of the Nile River and are known for being home to magnificent ancient Egyptian temples dating back nearly 4,000 years. Luxor alone has one-third of the world’s ancient monuments, and many Egyptians like to call Aswan “the Egyptian paradise.”

Safari in the desert

Desert safaris are the perfect way to discover the mysterious Arabian Desert, which occupies most of the Arabian Peninsula and is home to many animals such as the oryx and gazelle. Activities on desert trips range from light free driving to more adventurous driving through the sand dunes.
